Study Visa for US

Generally, a foreign national who wishes to enter the United States must first obtain a visa, either a nonimmigrant visa for temporary residence or an immigrant visa for permanent residence. You must have a student visa to study in the United States. Your course of study and the type of school you plan to attend will determine whether you need an F or M visa.

The following visa category is required to enter the United States: University or college High school Private elementary school Seminary Conservatory Other academic institution, including a language program Vocational or other recognized non-academic institution that is not a language program

  • F
  • M

Students cannot travel on a Visa Waiver Program or Visitor Visa, , United States- Student visa (F or M) is required to study. Foreign nationals are not eligible to study under a Visitor Visa (B) or Visa Waiver Program (VWP) except to complete recreational (non-credit) studies as part of a tourist visit.

Visitor visa (B) may be suitable for short leisure study. The Visitor Visa (B) allows you to enroll in a short recreational course that does not lead to a degree or academic certificate. Learn more about visitor visa. A visit to the United States for a degree or certificate is never permitted on a (B) visa, even if short-term. For example, a student in a distance learning program that requires some time on an institution's American campus must obtain a student visa (F or M) before entering the United States. Student Admission to an SEVP-Approved School

The first step is to apply to an SEVP-approved school in the United States. Once your enrollment has been accepted by an SEVP-approved school, you will be registered in the Student and Exchange Visitor Information System and must pay the SEVIS I-901 fee. An SEVP-approved school will give you a Form I-20. After you have received your Form I-20 and registered in the SEVIS system, you can apply for a student visa (F or M) at a US embassy or consulate. You must present Form I-20 to the consular officer when you attend the visa interview.

If your spouse and/or children plan to live with you in the United States while studying, they must also register with SEVIS, obtain an individual Form I-20 from an SEVP-approved school, and apply for a visa.

For more information about SEVIS and the SEVIS I-901 fee, visit the US Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) Student and Exchange Visitor Program (SEVP) website.

Visit the US State Department of Education website for information on undergraduate and graduate opportunities and an overview of the application process. You can also visit the DHS Study in State school search page to search for SEVP-certified schools.
